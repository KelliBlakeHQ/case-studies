The Work

One event.
A door that stayed open for years.

The Challenge

A fundraiser with no infrastructure behind it

Scheck Hillel had an annual fundraising event but no sponsorship framework to build on. The goal wasn't just to run a successful event — it was to use the event to open a door the organization could walk through for years afterward.

The Approach

Build the systems, not just the event

The fundraiser became the entry point for introducing a formal sponsorship concept to school leadership. Alongside the event itself, a 300-page operations handbook was designed and a sponsorship department was launched — giving the school the tools to sustain and grow what was started.

Work Delivered

Event, infrastructure,
and a blueprint for what came next

01
Annual Fundraiser Production
Planned and executed the school's annual fundraising event end-to-end — delivered under budget with all goals met. The event served as the proof of concept for a larger sponsorship strategy.
02
Sponsorship Department Launch
Introduced the concept of formal corporate sponsorship to school leadership and built the foundational infrastructure for a dedicated sponsorship department — the first of its kind at the school.
03
300-Page Operations Handbook
Designed a comprehensive operations and event handbook that codified every process, role, and standard — ensuring the work could be replicated and scaled by whoever came next.
"The work wasn't just to run a successful event — it was to make sure the event opened a door the organization could walk through for years."
