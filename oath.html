The Work

The goal wasn't to manage the crisis —

The Challenge

A global tour mid-crisis, mid-merger

Oath's global photobooth tour — a large-scale employee experience program spanning 50+ cities across the US and EMEA — hit simultaneous crises: a major agency merger and a critical software failure. The tour couldn't stop. The client couldn't know.

The Approach

Absorb the chaos. Protect the experience.

Every backend disruption was handled before it could surface to the client or participants. New communications infrastructure was built on the fly via Slack. Software issues were diagnosed and resolved in real time. The participant experience remained seamless from start to finish.

Work Delivered

Four workstreams.
Zero client-facing disruptions.

01
Global Scheduling & Logistics
Managed end-to-end scheduling and logistics coordination across 50+ cities in the US and EMEA — maintaining accuracy and continuity throughout the agency merger disruption.
02
Software QA & Crisis Management
Identified, diagnosed, and resolved critical software failures in real time — preventing any disruption from reaching participants or the client while the backend was stabilized.
03
Communications Infrastructure
Built a Slack-based communications system from scratch to keep the distributed team coordinated across time zones, regions, and organizational chaos — replacing the infrastructure lost in the merger.
04
White-Glove Participant Experience
Maintained the white-glove participant experience Oath had promised its employees — ensuring that 15,000+ people across two regions had no awareness of the operational complexity happening behind the scenes.
